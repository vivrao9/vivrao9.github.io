My favorite projects

idsnews.com [redesign]

Led a team on strict deadline and learned a lot about project management by asking questions like “How much padding should this get?” or “What font should we use?” Tested demos and collaborated with the newsroom to find those answers.

Our focus was on making branding more consistent, optimizing ad revenue, updating fonts, and giving web layouts a facelift. Take a look at the files here.

Role: web design, leadership, project management

Skills: Adobe InDesign, responsive web layouts


Tracking coronavirus cases in Indiana

Created and managed Indiana's first coronavirus tracker. Our staff did this by setting up a Slack channel to crowdsource county reports and announcements — became our most read story from the spring semester.

Later automated the map by setting up a cron job to collect data and update a CARTO map daily on strict deadline.

Role: updated daily on strict deadline, visualization, shared to social platforms

Skills: automation using Python, cartography, data visualization


IU found a professor sexually harassed a freshman, allowed him to continue teaching

Designed and developed a mobile-first longform piece that helped tell the story of how an IU professor was allowed to teach despite exhibiting “a concerning pattern of singling out certain students” and sexually harassing them.

This piece taught me the importance of planning and coordination as I worked on the web product from a different time zone. Our team prioritized timeliness, communication and frequent check-ins.

Role: web design, development

Skills: HTML, CSS, working on deadline


S for Success: Why IU students’ spring 2020 grades were higher despite COVID-19

Analyzed grade distribution data to determine the impact of the new 'S' grade policy on overall grades. Interviewed students, faculty and administrators.

Worked on digital production by incorporating scrollytelling and graphics to craft longform presentation.

Role: data analysis, writing, visualizations

Skills: HTML, CSS, Excel, scrollytelling, data visualization, interviewing


Utilities analysis finds science buildings use most electricity, and other key takeaways

Analyzed and visualized IU's utilities data and, in the process, discovered a broken fountain wasting millions of gallons of water.

For this story, I learned how to ask hard questions. I was at the university's physical plant at least twice a week to press the assistant director on how he hadn't discovered the leak.

My favorite memory was biking for an hour to manually find buildings whose addresses could not be geocoded.

Role: Data analysis, interviewing, visualizations, writing

Skills: data wrangling, JavaScript, geocoding, Mapbox


Clean freaks, night owls and Laundrygate: How IU does its laundry

Built a web scraper to collect laundry use data across residence halls on campus to determine the optimal time to do laundry. Created a D3 heatmap to visualize this data.

Set up my first slackbot (Laundry Larry) to alert enterprise team if there was an issue while scraping.

This story taught me to be efficient in my work — since my computer was continuously running the scraper at home, I had to complete homework on campus computers before the library closed every evening.

Role: data analysis, web scraping, visualizations

Skills: analysis, cron jobs, JavaScript/Plotly, Python


Pandemic playbook: How UITS took IU online

Interviewed university technology center to write a story about how they planned for and executed IU's move to online learning in the wake of the coronavirus pandemic. Wrote copy and designed graphics for print and social media. Read the sequel here.

Role: SEO, data analysis and visualizations, writing

Skills: interviewing, visualization, working on deadline


TL;DR on Monroe County’s June 2 Indiana primary candidates

Role: web design, research, interviewing, illustration

Skills: HTML, CSS, JQuery, Adobe Illustrator, working on deadline
